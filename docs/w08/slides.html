The Present-Day Policy Framework (Descriptive): Notice and Consent

OECD Guidelines, 1980

  • ‚ÄúThe basis for most modern privacy laws‚Äù (Sugimoto, Ekbia, and Mattioli 2016)
  • Collection Limitation Principle: data may be collected ‚Äúwhere appropriate, with the knowledge or consent of the data subject.‚Äù (OECD 1980, 14)
  • Use Limitation Principle: ‚ÄúPersonal data should not be disclosed, made available or otherwise used for purposes other than those specified [at time of collection] except with the consent of the data subject‚Äù (OECD 1980, 15)

EU Data Protection Directive, 1995

  • Art. 7: Processing allowed when ‚Äúthe data subject has unambiguously given his [sic] consent.‚Äù
  • Art. 8: Use of sensitive data is restricted, except where ‚Äúthe data subject has given his [sic] explicit consent to the processing of those data.‚Äù
  • Art. 26: Prohibits export of personal data to non-European countries lacking ‚Äúadequate data protection‚Äù, except when ‚Äúthe data subject has given his [sic] consent unambiguously to the proposed transfer‚Äù (European Union 1995)
